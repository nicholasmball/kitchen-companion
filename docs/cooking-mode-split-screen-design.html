Split-Screen Cooking Mode

UX Design Document — Building on Requirements & Acceptance Criteria (Step 1)

Design Rationale
Problem: Currently, cooking mode renders ingredients and instructions sequentially. Users must scroll back up to check ingredients while following steps — frustrating with messy hands.

Solution: A split-screen layout (per R1) with sticky ingredients on the left and scrollable step checklist on the right. This follows the proven pattern noted in the Design Guide: "Recipe views work best with two columns on tablets (ingredients left, steps right)".

Key decisions:
• 35/65 split (R1) — ingredients need less width; instructions benefit from more reading space
• Left panel scrolls independently if ingredients overflow, but stays fixed in position
• Ingredient checkboxes — tap to tick off ingredients as you use them. Green checkbox (distinct from orange step checkboxes). Checked items get strikethrough + fade. Progress counter "4 of 12 ingredients used" shown above the list.
• Subtle background gradient on ingredients panel (R5) differentiates the two zones without a heavy visual border
• Active step highlight (R5 nice-to-have) — included as it helps users track position after looking away
• Mobile collapsed bar (R2) shows ingredient progress "4/12 used" when collapsed
• Component extraction (R4) into components/recipes/cooking-mode.tsx
User Flow
1
Enter Cooking Mode
Tap "Cooking Mode" button on recipe page
→
2
Split Layout Loads
Ingredients left, steps right. Wake lock activates.
→
3
Cook & Check Off
Scroll steps, tap to check off. Ingredients always visible.
→
4
Complete / Exit
All steps done! Exit returns to normal view.
Desktop / Tablet Layout (≥768px)
Layout: display: flex container below the banner. Left panel has position: sticky behavior via flex + independent overflow-y: auto. Right panel scrolls independently.
Tailwind classes: flex h-[calc(100vh-8rem)] md:flex-row flex-col
Breakpoint: md: (768px) — below this, switches to stacked mobile layout.

Servings

✓

✓
3 cloves garlic, crushed
✓
2 sprigs fresh rosemary
1 kg Maris Piper potatoes, peeled and quartered
500 g carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
300 g parsnips, peeled and halved
200 g Yorkshire pudding batter
4 tbsp goose fat
500 ml beef stock
2 tbsp plain flour
to taste salt and pepper
Instructions
3 of 8 steps done
✓
Step 1
Preheat the oven to 220°C (200°C fan). Take the beef out of the fridge 30 minutes before cooking to bring to room temperature.
✓
Step 2
Rub the beef with olive oil, salt, pepper, and crushed garlic. Place rosemary sprigs on top and put in a roasting tin.
✓
Step 3
Roast for 20 minutes at 220°C, then reduce to 170°C (150°C fan) for 1 hour for medium. Baste every 20 minutes.
Step 4
Meanwhile, parboil the potatoes for 8 minutes. Drain and shake in the colander to roughen the edges. Toss with 2 tbsp goose fat.
Step 5
Heat remaining goose fat in a separate roasting tin at 220°C. Add potatoes carefully, turning to coat. Roast for 45-50 minutes, turning halfway.
Step 6
Toss carrots and parsnips with olive oil, salt and pepper. Add to oven 35 minutes before the beef is done.
Step 7
Remove beef from oven, cover loosely with foil, and rest for 20 minutes. Increase oven to 220°C and cook Yorkshire puddings for 15-18 minutes.
Step 8
Make gravy: place roasting tin on hob, add flour and stir for 1 minute. Gradually add beef stock, stirring to deglaze. Simmer until thickened. Season to taste.

Desktop view — 35/65 split, both panels scroll independently, ingredients always in view

sticky ingredients ingredient checkboxes (green) border-right separator active step highlight progress counters on both panels independent scroll large tap targets (44px+ rows)
Mobile Layout (<768px)
Stacked layout: Collapsible ingredients bar sticks below the cooking banner. Tap to expand/collapse.
Collapsed state: Shows "12 ingredients — tap to view" with a chevron. Minimal height (~44px) to maximise step visibility.
Expanded state: Ingredients list with servings adjuster, max-height 50vh with its own scroll, overlays instructions.
Tailwind: md:hidden for mobile-only panel, hidden md:flex for desktop split.

Component Architecture
New component: components/recipes/cooking-mode.tsx

Props interface:
recipe: Recipe — full recipe data
servings: number — current adjusted servings
onServingsChange: (n: number) => void
completedSteps: Set<number>
onToggleStep: (index: number) => void
checkedIngredients: Set<number>
onToggleIngredient: (index: number) => void
onExit: () => void

Internal state:
ingredientsExpanded: boolean — mobile collapsible panel (default: false)

Responsive strategy:
• Use md:flex-row / flex-col for the split — single component, responsive via Tailwind
• Mobile collapsible panel rendered with md:hidden
• Desktop side panel rendered with hidden md:block
• Height: h-[calc(100vh-10rem)] accounting for navbar + banner + title
Accessibility Notes
• All existing ARIA attributes preserved: role="checkbox", aria-checked, aria-label on steps
• Ingredient checkboxes: role="checkbox", aria-checked, aria-label="1.5 kg beef topside joint"
• Collapsible ingredients panel: role="region", aria-expanded, aria-controls
• Toggle button: aria-label="Show ingredients" / "Hide ingredients"
• Keyboard: Enter/Space toggles ingredients panel on mobile, step checkboxes unchanged
• Focus management: after collapsing panel, focus returns to toggle button
• Minimum touch targets: 44x44px maintained for all interactive elements
• Contrast: all text/background combinations verified against project palette (WCAG AA)
